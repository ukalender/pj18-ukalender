Football Data Analysis: How Big European Leagues Differs and What Drives Success?¶

Introduction¶

Football industry has been grown fast and attractiveness of the game becomes more important in order to survive in such a financially-hard-to-survive environment. You should keep fans to support you, sponsors to finance you and players to prefer your.

So how key figures differ in 5 big leagues? What are the main differences between them? Analyzing the key data on these leagues can give some answers to get overview of statistics from these these 5 leagues and how leadership journey is competitive across them.

Explanation of dataset directly from kaggle:

The dataset provides a granular view of 9,074 games, totaling 941,009 events from the biggest 5 European football (soccer) leagues: England, Spain, Germany, Italy, France from 2011/2012 season to 2016/2017 season as of 25.01.2017. There are games that have been played during these seasons for which I could not collect detailed data. Overall, over 90% of the played games during these seasons have event data.

The dataset is organized in 3 files:

events.csv contains event data about each game. Text commentary was scraped from: bbc.com, espn.com and onefootball.com ginf.csv - contains metadata and market odds about each game. odds were collected from oddsportal.com dictionary.txt contains a dictionary with the textual description of each categorical variable coded with integers [https://www.kaggle.com/secareanualin/football-events/home]

How Distribution of Goal Minutes Differ per League¶

Although goal per game figures are close to each other, it's important to look at distribution of occurence of these goals during the full time of 90 minutes game.

We have this info within our game_events dataset:

  • is_goal show that event ended up with scoring or not (1 means that event is finalized as goal)
  • time indicates that event occured at which minute of the game

It would be useful to break the 90 minutes into time bands rather than looking at discrete values. I think 20 minute breaks would be enough. So our time brackets will be:

  • 0-20 min
  • 20-40 min
  • 40-60 min
  • 60-80 min
  • 80+ min

Now, what are steps that we need to complete to get this insight from the dataset:

  • filter game_events datset to include only rows where is_goal variable is equal to 1
  • define new variable which assign 5 time brackes given above to minute variable of original dataset
  • group by country and time brackets and count the goals for each
  • visualize the results by stacked bar chart

Conclusion¶

Most of the football fans claim that England Premier Leagues is most attractive league in terms of attractiveness of the game and war of physical struggle. Ball is fastly passing middle of the pitch and there are a lot of attacks per match. Our selective dataset shows that Bundesliga has higher goal per match figure than England Premier League but do not forget that our data is not comprehensive enough to claim something bold.

Additionaly we can say that key figures more or less sames across 5 big leagues: England, Spain, France, Italy and Gremany. For instance distribution of goals per minutes peaks between 40th and 60th minutes for most of the leagues, only Germany (Bundesliga) differs with peak at 60th - 80th minute range.

Finally, it's clear that as minutes pass players become more aggressive as time distribution of yellow and red cards indicate. Maybe it can be associated with ease of taking risk when there is less minutes to play. It's more clear in red cards graph especially. Most of the red cards are shown after 70th minute and peaks after 80th minute. Extra minutes would probably dominate there.
